## Neuschwanstein: Real or Fairy Tale?

Seeing it for the first time is hard to believe. Clinging to a mountainside in the middle of the Alps — pink and white, like fiction. But it was completed in 1886, by order of the eccentric King Ludwig II.

Disney used this castle as inspiration for Sleeping Beauty's palace. But Disney's copy can't surpass the original.

## Getting There

**Train:** Munich → Füssen, Regional Express, ~2 hours. Deutschlandticket valid.

**From Füssen to the castle:**
- **Bus:** Lines 73 or 78 to Schwangau Schlösser stop. ~10 min.
- **Taxi:** Direct from Füssen station.

**From Schwangau to the castle:**
- **Walking:** ~30–40 minutes, uphill
- **Horse carriage:** Ticketed, long queues in busy seasons
- **Shuttle bus:** Takes you close to the top

---

## Tickets: The Most Critical Point

**Buy online in advance** — not just a cliché here, genuinely essential. Daily visitor numbers are limited. In summer, days can sell out well in advance.

**Where:** [www.ticket-center-hohenschwangau.de](https://www.ticket-center-hohenschwangau.de)

**Price (2025):** Adult ~15 EUR, children (0–17) free.

**Booking fee:** 2.50 EUR extra for online — worth it.

**Important:** You must arrive at the ticket counter at the time specified on your ticket. Being 5 minutes late for your tour time means your ticket is invalid.

---

## Inside the Castle Tour

Ticket includes: guided interior tour, approximately 35 minutes. Audio guide available in multiple languages.

**Highlights:** Throne Room (Tronsaal), bedroom and study, Grotto (artificial cave), Knights' Hall.

Ludwig II could only use this castle for 172 nights before his death in 1886.

---

## Marienbrücke: Best Viewpoint

The bridge near the castle — this is actually where you need to go for photos. From the bridge you can see both the castle and the valley — the classic Neuschwanstein photos you see online are taken from here.

**How to get there:** 10 minutes walk from the castle, uphill.

**Warning:** Bridge may be closed in winter. Check before going.

**Tip:** Go to Marienbrücke early morning — cloudless sky and fewer people.

---

## Tips to Avoid Crowds

1.5 million visitors per year. Overwhelming crowds at summer midday. To avoid:

1. **Book the first tour slot** — opens at 09:00, first tours are quietest
2. **Prefer weekdays** — weekends much more crowded
3. **Visit in April or October** — not as crowded as summer
4. **Walk up instead of taking the bus** — skip the bus queue

---

## Hohenschwangau Castle: The Other Option

Directly opposite Neuschwanstein, on the lower hill — another castle, Hohenschwangau. Ludwig II spent his childhood here. Less crowded, much more authentic interior.

Sold as a combined package ticket with Neuschwanstein. See it if you have time.

---

## With Stroller

Castle interior cannot be visited with a stroller — they don't allow them inside. Leave it at the ticket office (free storage). The uphill walk to the castle is steep — horse carriage or shuttle bus is a sensible option.

---

## Practical Information

**How long?** Castle tour 35 minutes. Allow 4–5 hours including travel and waiting. Add Marienbrücke: 5–6 hours.

**Food:** Small shops near the castle entrance. No large restaurant — bring food.

**Best season:** May–June and September–October. July–August most crowded.

---

## FAQ

**Can Neuschwanstein tickets sell out?** Yes, in summer they can sell out days in advance. Buy online.

**Do children pay?** Ages 0–17 are free.

**Is overnight stay needed?** Not for just the castle. Day trip from Munich works if you leave early.

**Best spot for castle photos?** Marienbrücke bridge — the classic shot.
