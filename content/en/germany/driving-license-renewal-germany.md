## The Legal Framework: Turkish License in Germany

The clock starts ticking the moment you register your address (**Anmeldung**). A license issued in Turkey is considered non-EU, so it is **only valid for 6 months.**

After this period, there is no legal room for "it'll be fine" or "they won't check." Germany leaves no gray areas here.

### Which Country Licenses Are Directly Valid in Germany?

| License Country | Validity in Germany |
|---|---|
| EU member states | Valid indefinitely, no exchange required |
| Switzerland, Norway, Iceland | Directly valid |
| Turkey | **6 months** from Anmeldung |
| Ukraine, Serbia etc. | Depends on country |
| USA, Canada | Depends on state/province, usually 6 months |

## What Happens If You Miss the 6 Months?

- Driving becomes **legally prohibited**
- In the event of an accident, insurance may be considered **invalid**
- Car rental companies may request additional documents or refuse

This is why you should start researching fahrschules within the first week of settling in Germany.

## Step-by-Step: Getting a Driving License in Germany

### 1. Choosing a Fahrschule

Good fahrschules operate with waiting lists. In major cities like Munich and Hamburg, you may need to register months in advance.

**What to look for in a fahrschule:**
- Does it offer theory lessons in Turkish or English?
- What vehicles do they use for practical lessons?
- What is their average lesson count and exam pass rate?
- Are prices clearly stated?

### 2. Theory Test (Theorieprüfung)

- Total of **30 questions**, maximum **10 error points** allowed
- Can be taken in German, English or Turkish
- Official apps: **DEGENER** or **Führerschein 2025**
- Average study time: 4–8 weeks
- Exam fee: ~25 EUR

**Important:** Fahrschule registration and Anmeldung are required before taking the exam.

### 3. Practical Lessons

Driver training in Germany is very different from Turkey. The instructor will not send you to the exam until they consider you competent.

**Mandatory lesson types:**
- Regular city driving lessons
- **Autobahnfahrt** — motorway driving (mandatory)
- **Nachtfahrt** — night driving (mandatory)
- **Überlandfahrt** — country road driving (mandatory)

How many lessons you take depends entirely on the instructor. This directly affects the cost.

### 4. Practical Exam (Fahrprüfung)

- Exam duration: ~45 minutes
- Exam fee: ~200 EUR
- Failing on the first attempt is very common — you can retake it

| Item | Estimated Cost |

| Per lesson average | ~50–70 EUR |

| **Total average** | **2,000–3,500 EUR** |
| **Total duration** | **5–6 months** |

**City difference:** Smaller cities are generally cheaper. Munich and Hamburg are among the most expensive.

No, not to start. But it's required when registering for the exam.
