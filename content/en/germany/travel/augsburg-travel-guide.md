## Augsburg: The Unjustly Overlooked City

Most visitors to Munich skip Augsburg. Yet just 35 minutes away, a city with a far longer history — over 2,000 years of Roman heritage — is waiting.

**Fuggerei** alone is reason enough to come: the world's oldest social housing project, in continuous operation since 1516.

---

## Getting There

**Train:** Direct from Munich Hauptbahnhof, 30–40 minutes. Deutschlandticket valid. Multiple departures per hour.

---

## Places to Visit

### 1. Fuggerei — World's Oldest Social Housing Project
Built in 1516 by Jakob Fugger for the poor of Augsburg — and still used for the same purpose today. Residents pay just **88 cents a year** (the historical 1 Rhenish guilden). In return, they pray three times a day in the church — for the Fugger family.

Visit: Small museum open. WWII air raid shelter visible. Entry ~4 EUR.

### 2. Rathaus and Goldener Saal
Augsburg's symbol. 17th-century Renaissance masterpiece. Inside, the **Goldener Saal** (Golden Hall) is stunning: ceiling decorations, gold leaf, magnificent proportions. Destroyed in WWII, fully restored.

### 3. Augsburger Dom (Cathedral)
One of Germany's oldest churches. 11th century, with foundations going back even further. Most notable: **world's oldest figurative stained glass windows** — 11th century, still original.

### 4. Maximilianstrasse
Augsburg's main street, called one of Europe's most beautiful. Fugger palaces, grand churches and three large fountains (Mercury, Hercules, Augustus) line this street.

### 5. Water Management System
UNESCO World Heritage. Medieval water canal system. Canals visible throughout the city.

---

## Practical Information

**How long?** 4–5 hours for the center including Fuggerei.

**Stroller:** Center largely flat and comfortable. Canal areas have some cobblestones but generally good.

**Cheaper than Munich:** Rent is about half of Munich's. Many Munich workers live in Augsburg.

---

## FAQ

**How to get to Augsburg from Munich?** S-Bahn (S3) or Regional Express, 30–40 minutes. Deutschlandticket valid.

**Where is Fuggerei?** In the Jakobervorstadt neighborhood, 10–15 minutes walk from center.

**Is one day enough for Augsburg?** Yes, a full day is perfect. Leave early morning, return in the evening.
