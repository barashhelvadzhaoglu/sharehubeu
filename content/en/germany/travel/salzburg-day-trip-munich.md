## Salzburg: Munich's Nearest Foreign City

From Munich, Salzburg feels almost like a neighbor. A 1.5-hour train ride, free with Deutschlandticket to the border. A few euros more for the short stretch after Freilassing.

But when you arrive, you're in another country — Austria, with all its cultural weight: Mozart, Hohensalzburg and Sound of Music.

---

## Getting There

**Train:** Munich Hauptbahnhof → Freilassing (free with Deutschlandticket) → Salzburg Hauptbahnhof (separate ticket, ~4–6 EUR). Total ~1.5 hours. Direct service, no change needed — brief stop at Freilassing.

---

## Places to Visit

### 1. Hohensalzburg Fortress
Dominating the city from atop its hill, one of Europe's largest and best-preserved medieval fortresses. Dating from the 11th century.

**How to get up:** Walk (~20 min, uphill) or Funicular from Kapitel Square — sold as a package with fortress ticket. Funicular + fortress museum ~16 EUR adult.

**View:** Salzburg city, Salzach River and surrounding Alps — magnificent.

### 2. Getreidegasse
Salzburg's most famous street. Narrow, charming, decorated with the iron guild signs. Mozart was born here — Mozart Geburtshaus at Getreidegasse 9. Museum entry ~13 EUR.

**Tip:** Very crowded. Go early morning or late afternoon.

### 3. Mirabell Palace and Gardens
Free entry. Baroque gardens, manicured flower beds, views of Hohensalzburg. The "Do-Re-Mi" scene from Sound of Music was filmed here. Perfect for photos.

### 4. Altstadt (Old Town)
UNESCO World Heritage. Religious buildings, narrow streets, Residenzplatz. **Salzburger Dom** (Cathedral) is a must — the interior is incredible.

### 5. Kapuzinerberg
Small hill opposite Mirabell. Worth climbing for panoramic city views — free, 15–20 minute walk.

---

## Practical Information

**Currency:** Euro. **Language:** German, English very common in tourist areas.

**How long?** Full day — leave early morning, return in the evening. 5–7 hours on the ground is plenty.

**Stroller:** Altstadt partially cobblestone. Mirabell gardens perfect. Fortress accessible by funicular.

**Best season:** April–October. July–August very crowded. December Christmas markets are wonderful.

---

## FAQ

**Visa for Salzburg?** Schengen visa sufficient. Austria is in Schengen.

**Is Deutschlandticket valid to Salzburg?** Valid to Freilassing. Freilassing → Salzburg separate ticket (~4–6 EUR). Total very cheap.

**Is one day enough for Salzburg?** Yes for the city. Add Hallstatt or Salzkammergut for 2 days.
