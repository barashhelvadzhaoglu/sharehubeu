## Christmas in Germany: Two Separate Celebrations

In many countries "New Year's" means December 31st. In Germany, winter celebrations are split into two distinct periods:

**Weihnachten (Christmas) — December 24-26:** Family gatherings, quiet celebration, gift-giving. Germany's most important holiday of the year.

**Silvester — December 31st:** New Year's Eve. Fireworks, champagne, friend gatherings.

The two carry very different energy. And for someone who has just moved to Germany, both come with surprises.

---

## Weihnachten: German Christmas Culture

### Christmas Is a Family Holiday

In Germany, Christmas is entirely family-focused. December 24th evening (Heiligabend — Holy Night) is the most important moment. Dinner, opening gifts and usually church. Most restaurants and shops are closed.

For newcomers, this can be a surprise: the city essentially empties out from the afternoon of December 24th. Supermarkets close, metro runs less frequently.

**Practical tip:** Finish your shopping on December 23rd. December 24th midday is the last chance.

### Advent: The Build-Up to Christmas

In Germany, Christmas doesn't start on December 24th — it starts with Advent. Advent covers the four weeks before Christmas.

**Adventskranz:** A wreath with four candles. One candle is lit each Sunday.
**Adventskalender:** A calendar with a daily surprise. Chocolate versions are very popular for children.

## Munich Christmas Markets: Christkindlmarkt

Munich's Christmas market is one of the most famous in the world. The main market at Marienplatz has been running since 1310.

**Main markets:**

| Market | Location | Character |
|---|---|---|
| Christkindlmarkt | Marienplatz | Largest, most traditional |
| Mittelaltermarkt | Wittelsbacherplatz | Medieval theme |
| Schwabing Markt | Münchner Freiheit | More local, calmer |
| Haidhauser Markt | Weißenburger Platz | Hipster, alternative |
| Tollwood | Olympiapark | Festival atmosphere, organic |

**Opening dates:** Usually late November to December 24th.

### What to Eat and Drink at the Christmas Market?

**Glühwein:** Hot spiced wine. Can be red or white. In Munich, the mug price is usually 4-6 EUR (including mug deposit).

**Kinderpunsch:** Non-alcoholic Glühwein version, perfect for children.

**Lebkuchen:** Christmas spice cookies. The large heart-shaped ones make great souvenirs.

**Bratwurst:** Grilled sausage with mustard. A Christmas market essential.

**Gebrannte Mandeln:** Caramelized almonds. Their aroma fills the market.

### Christmas Market with a Stroller

During busy periods (weekends, especially the last week of December) the main Marienplatz market gets very crowded. Pushing a stroller can become difficult.

**Recommendation:** Go on a weekday morning or during the first week of the market — both calmer and more atmospheric.

## Silvester: A Night Beyond Expectations

For someone experiencing Silvester (December 31st) in Germany for the first time, the biggest surprise is: **fireworks.**

In Germany, individual fireworks sales are permitted for Silvester. From midnight onwards — and even before — fireworks are launched from everywhere. Looking out the window, the city seems to turn into a war zone.

This is very different from New Year's celebrations elsewhere. Louder, more chaotic, more unexpected.

### Where to Celebrate Silvester in Munich?

**Marienplatz:** Thousands gather, fireworks display at midnight.
**Englischer Garten:** More free-spirited, picnic atmosphere.
**City bars and restaurants:** Reservation essential, usually special menus and prices.

**Celebrating at home:** Many German families celebrate Silvester at home with close friends. Making Raclette is very popular — a tabletop grill with cheese and various toppings. A slow and enjoyable evening.

### If You Have Pets

Silvester fireworks affect pets most of all. In Germany, this is a genuine problem. Veterinarians can prescribe sedatives in advance. If you have a pet, be prepared.

## Christmas Shopping

**Gifts at the Weihnachtsmarkt:** Handmade products, toys, decorations. Prices are high but quality is good.

**Post-Christmas sales:** From December 26th, big sales start in Germany. Shopping after Christmas is very sensible.

**Online shopping:** Amazon.de gets very slow in November-December. Order during Black Friday to ensure delivery by Christmas.

## Practical Information

**Public holidays:**
- December 24 (Heiligabend): Half day
- December 25-26: Official holiday, most things closed
- December 31 (Silvester): Half day
- January 1: Official holiday

**Public transport:** Runs infrequently on Christmas days (December 25-26) and January 1st. Plan ahead.

**Silvester taxis:** The hardest to find and most expensive night of the year. Celebrate somewhere walkable or book well in advance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How many days is the Christmas holiday in Germany?**
December 25-26 are official holidays. Many workplaces also close from December 24th afternoon.

**When does the Munich Christmas market open?**
Usually from the last week of November to December 24th. Exact dates are announced each year.

**Is there a non-alcoholic version of Glühwein?**
Yes, it's available as Kinderpunsch or alkoholfreier Glühwein.

**Can I buy fireworks for Silvester?**
Yes, they go on sale between December 28-31. However, some areas have restrictions — check your local area.

**Are there places to visit in Munich on Christmas Day?**
Most museums and places are closed December 24-26. The Christmas market is open until midday on December 24th. The Englischer Garten is always open.
