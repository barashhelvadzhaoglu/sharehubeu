## Ingolstadt: Beyond Audi

When people hear Ingolstadt, Audi comes to mind — the headquarters is here. But the historic center tells a completely different story: the Danube River, medieval walls and unexpected museums make for a small but rich city.

1 hour from Munich, free with Deutschlandticket.

---

## Getting There

**Train:** Regional Express from Munich Hauptbahnhof, ~1 hour. Deutschlandticket valid. Frequent departures.

---

## Places to Visit

### 1. Altstadt and City Walls
Ingolstadt's medieval walls are largely preserved. You can walk along them. **Kreuztor** — the city's most beautiful gate, 14th century.

### 2. Neues Schloss (New Palace) and Army Museum
Named "new" but dating from the 15th century. Inside is the **Bayerisches Armeemuseum** (Bavarian Army Museum) — one of Germany's largest military museums. Entry ~5 EUR.

### 3. Liebfrauenmünster (Church)
Ingolstadt's most important religious building. 14th–15th century Gothic church. The frescoes and stained glass inside are impressive.

### 4. Danube (Donau) Riverside
The Danube flows beside the city. Walking and cycling paths along the bank. Green spaces, picnic spots. The city's most peaceful and beautiful section.

### 5. The Frankenstein Connection
An interesting historical detail: Mary Shelley chose Ingolstadt University as the place where Doctor Frankenstein worked when writing Frankenstein. There are signs and small monuments around the city referencing this connection.

### 6. Audi Forum (Optional)
~10 minutes from the city center. Car museum, factory tours (advance booking required). Interesting for car enthusiasts but not essential for a city visit.

---

## Practical Information

**How long?** 3–4 hours for the center. Add Audi Forum for 5–6 hours.

**Stroller:** Center largely flat and modern. Danube riverside excellent. Historic Altstadt partially cobblestone.

---

## FAQ

**How to get to Ingolstadt from Munich?** Regional Express, ~1 hour. Deutschlandticket valid.

**Is the Audi factory tour free?** Museum is free but factory tour is paid and requires booking.

**How long to spend in Ingolstadt?** Half a day for the city. Full day possible.
