## Lindau: An Island City in the Middle of a Lake

On the Bavarian side of Lake Constance (Bodensee), built on a small island connected to the mainland by a bridge. The moment you step into the old town, the lake surrounds you and Austrian mountains rise across the water.

This view exists nowhere else.

---

## Getting There

**Train:** Direct Regional Express from Munich Hauptbahnhof. About 2 hours. Deutschlandticket valid. The station is on the island — you're already in the center.

---

## Places to Visit

### 1. Harbor (Hafen) and Lion Statue
Lindau's most iconic image: the Bavarian lion statue at the harbor entrance, opposite the lighthouse. Austrian mountains in the background. Perfect for photos — less crowded early morning or evening.

Boat rental and lake tours available (separately priced).

### 2. Altstadt (Old Town)
Colorful houses with balconies, flower-decorated narrow streets. Medieval texture almost entirely preserved. Walking around is an experience in itself.

### 3. Mangturm (Mang Tower)
Medieval lighthouse tower, symbol of the harbor. Particularly beautiful in late afternoon sun.

### 4. Lake Walk
Circling the island takes about 30–40 minutes. Lake views from every direction, Austrian and Swiss mountains visible. Excellent for strollers — completely flat.

### 5. Swimming in Lake Constance
In summer, the lake is great for swimming. Beaches around the island. Very clean water.

---

## Three Country Feature

Lindau is very close to where three countries share Lake Constance — Germany, Austria and Switzerland. Bregenz (Austria) is just across the lake — very close by boat.

---

## Practical Information

**How long?** 3–4 hours for the island. Full day possible adding lake tours or Bregenz.

**Stroller:** Island completely flat — ideal. Harbor walk, Altstadt, surroundings — all comfortable.

**Best season:** May–September. Summer can be crowded. Spring and autumn quieter.

---

## FAQ

**How to get to Lindau from Munich?** Regional Express direct, ~2 hours. Deutschlandticket valid.

**Can you cross to Austria from Lindau?** Yes. Bregenz is very close by boat or train. Extra ticket needed.

**Is there swimming in Lindau?** Yes, several swimming spots around the lake in summer.
