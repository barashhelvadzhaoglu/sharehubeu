## What Is the Deutschlandticket?

A subscription that started in 2023 and changed the lives of Germany residents: for **49 EUR per month**, you can use all local and regional public transport. S-Bahn, U-Bahn, tram, bus, regional trains — all included.

**What's NOT included:**
- ICE and IC trains (high-speed)
- Private bus lines
- Austria and Switzerland (ends at the border — but you can reach nearby cities very cheaply)

For someone Munich-based, this ticket is an incredible tool for exploring the surroundings. We tested this with two kids aged 1 and 5, and that famous double stroller.

---

## Deutschlandticket with Children

**How many children travel free?** Children under 6 are free across Germany — no Deutschlandticket needed. Ages 6–14 in Bavaria can often travel free with Bavarian tickets — but this rule varies with the Deutschlandticket, so check.

**Stroller:** Generally no problem on regional trains and S-Bahn — special wagons available. But some smaller stations have no elevator. Research routes beforehand.

## Top 7 Day Trip Routes from Munich

### 1. Füssen and Neuschwanstein Castle
**Duration:** ~2 hours (Regional Express, direct)
**Cost:** Free with Deutschlandticket

Bavaria's and perhaps Germany's most iconic destination. The pink and white turreted castle in front of mountain scenery. The castle that inspired Disney.

**Practical info:**
- **Buy entry tickets online in advance** — daily visitor limit applies
- Bus from Füssen station to the castle (not included, separate ticket)
- For strollers: inside the castle is stepped, outside is fine
- Go early morning — overwhelming crowds at midday

**How many hours with kids?** Full day — 8–10 hours

---

### 2. Garmisch-Partenkirchen
**Duration:** ~1.5 hours (direct)
**Cost:** Free with Deutschlandticket

At the foot of Germany's highest mountain, the Zugspitze. Winter ski resort, summer hiking routes.

**Things to do:**
- Partnach Gorge (Partnachklamm) — mountain valley walk, very impressive
- Zugspitze summit (separately priced, 50–60 EUR/person)
- Walk around the town center

**For strollers:** OK up to the Partnachklamm entrance — you can't enter inside, but the riverside walk is an alternative.

---

### 3. Starnberg Lake (Starnberger See)
**Duration:** ~30 minutes (S6 line)
**Cost:** Free with Deutschlandticket

Munich's closest day trip destination. Walk from the station to the lake. Perfect for swimming in summer.

**Practical info:**
- Starnberg, Tutzing, Pöcking — you can get off at different stations
- The lakeside walking path is completely flat — ideal for strollers
- Lots of picnic areas
- Boat tours available (separate fee)

**How many hours with kids?** Half day is enough, full day also works

---

### 4. Salzburg (Austria)
**Duration:** ~1.5 hours (Regional Express)
**Cost:** Deutschlandticket to the border, separate ticket from Freilassing (~5–10 EUR)

Technically Austria but no city is this close to Munich anywhere else. Mozart's birthplace, Hohensalzburg Fortress, Getreidegasse.

**Important:** Deutschlandticket ends at the German border. Freilassing → Salzburg requires a separate ticket — very cheap.

**For strollers:** Old town is partly cobblestone but manageable. Castle access has an elevator.

---

### 5. Chiemsee (Chiem Lake)
**Duration:** ~1 hour (direct)
**Cost:** Free with Deutschlandticket

Bavaria's largest lake. On an island is Ludwig II's unfinished palace Herrenchiemsee.

**Practical info:**
- Get off at Prien am Chiemsee station
- Boat to the island from there (separate fee ~10 EUR)
- Palace museum additionally priced
- Beautiful lakeside walks

---

### 6. Augsburg
**Duration:** ~35 minutes (direct)
**Cost:** Free with Deutschlandticket

Bavaria's second largest city after Munich. Roman-era historic center, Fuggerei (world's oldest social housing project), beautiful cathedrals.

**With kids:** Entirely within walking distance, flat streets — ideal for strollers.

---

### 7. Berchtesgaden
**Duration:** ~2.5 hours (with connection)
**Cost:** Free with Deutschlandticket

A natural wonder surrounded by the Alps. Königssee (King's Lake) can be toured by electric boat. Hitler's Eagle's Nest is also here — historical tours available.

**For strollers:** Königssee boat pier is flat. Eagle's Nest access by bus then walk/elevator.

## Useful Tools for Planning Routes

**DB Navigator app** — Deutsche Bahn's official app. Shows Deutschlandticket-compatible routes. Essential.

**Bayern Fahrplan** — Bavaria-specific train and bus timetables.

**Google Maps** — Good for planning routes with public transport option.

## What We Learned Traveling with a Stroller

We've been in Munich for over a year, exploring Bavaria with the Deutschlandticket and a double stroller. What we learned:

**Station selection:** Large stations generally have elevators. Small village stations often only have stairs. You can see elevator status in DB Navigator.

**Train selection:** Regional Express trains are spacious. S-Bahn can be crowded during peak hours. After 9 AM and after 3 PM is more comfortable.

**Mountain routes:** Hiking trails generally aren't suitable for strollers — but lakesides, town centers and most tourist areas are flat.

**Time planning:** Everything takes longer with two small children. Leave early for day trips, bring packed lunch, plan the return around children's sleep time.

## How to Get the Deutschlandticket?

- **DB App** (Deutsche Bahn)
- **MVV App** (Munich public transport)
- Any S-Bahn or U-Bahn machine
- Through Postbank or some banks

Monthly cancellable subscription — no long-term commitment.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I take ICE trains with the Deutschlandticket?**
No. Only local and regional trains — S-Bahn, U-Bahn, RB, RE, IRE. ICE and IC need a separate ticket.

**How many people can use one ticket?**
Each ticket is personal. People traveling with you need their own tickets. Children under 6 are free.

**Any difference at weekends?**
No, valid 24/7.

**Can I bring my bike?**
Varies by time. Bikes are prohibited during peak weekday hours. Check DB Navigator.

**Austria and Switzerland?**
Valid to the border. Additional ticket needed for cross-border travel — but very cheap.
