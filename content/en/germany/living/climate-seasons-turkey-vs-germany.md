# Climate & Seasons: Turkey vs Germany

*I knew German winters would be grey. I didn't know how grey. Or how beautiful the summers would be.*

---

## 🇹🇷 Turkey: Sun as Default

Turkey's climate varies dramatically by region — the Aegean and Mediterranean coasts offer warm, dry summers and mild winters; the Black Sea region is lush and rainy; central Anatolia gets genuine cold snaps in winter.

But the general experience of Turkish climate, especially in major western cities, is of **sun as the default setting**. Long summers, abundant light, warm evenings that invite outdoor life well into October. The relationship between weather and social life is intimate — people spill out onto streets, sit in tea gardens, stay outdoors late.

Winters in cities like Istanbul can be grey and damp, but they're short. Spring arrives decisively. The seasonal rhythm is clear and generally kind.

---

## 🇩🇪 Germany (Bavaria): Four Real Seasons

Munich sits in southern Bavaria, close to the Alps — which gives it a climate that's more varied and dramatic than the rest of Germany.

**Winters** are cold, often grey, and can feel very long. November through February brings limited daylight, frequent cloud cover, and temperatures that hover around freezing. Snow is possible but not always reliable in the city itself; the mountains nearby, however, get excellent snow.

**Spring** in Munich is genuinely beautiful — cherry blossoms, the English Garden coming alive, the city's famous *Frühlingsfest* and eventually the outdoor beer garden season opening.

**Summer** is magnificent. Long days, warm temperatures, the *Isar* river for swimming, beer gardens full until 11pm, people on bikes everywhere. Munich summers are among the best urban summer experiences in Europe.

**Autumn** in Bavaria is *Herbst* — golden, crisp, Oktoberfest, pumpkins, fog rolling in from the Alps. It's spectacular before it turns grey.

**Seasonal Affective Disorder** is a real phenomenon here. The grey months affect people. Vitamin D deficiency is common. Light therapy lamps are sold everywhere. Acknowledging this is not weakness — it's climate literacy.

## 💬 My Take

The grey of German winter was harder than I expected. Not the cold — the cold I could dress for. The absence of light, day after day, wore on me in ways I hadn't anticipated.

But Munich summers converted me. The combination of long days, Alpine backdrop, the *Isar*, and the city's outdoor culture makes summer here genuinely joyful in a way I hadn't experienced before.

And being one hour from ski slopes — that was an unexpected bonus. My relationship with seasons has completely changed. I now have a relationship with *winter* in a way I never did in Turkey.
