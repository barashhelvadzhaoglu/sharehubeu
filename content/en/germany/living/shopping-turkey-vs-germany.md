# Shopping & Daily Errands: Turkey vs Germany

*Sunday everything is closed. Yes, really. Here's how to adapt — and what you might actually prefer.*

---

## 🇹🇷 Turkey: Open, Loud, and Always Available

Shopping in Turkey is a full sensory experience. Markets are open late, corner shops (*bakkal*) are open almost around the clock, and if you run out of something at 11pm, there's almost always somewhere to go.

Bazaars, weekly street markets (*pazar*), and fresh produce shops create a rhythm to the week that feels alive. Prices are negotiable in many contexts. Personal relationships with shopkeepers matter — your local *bakkal* might extend you credit, remember your usual order, and ask about your family.

Big shopping malls have also exploded across Turkish cities — open seven days a week, late hours, full of food courts and entertainment. Convenience is built into the culture.

The downside: price consistency is hard to rely on, especially as inflation has made costs unpredictable. And the sheer volume of choice and noise can be overwhelming.

---

## 🇩🇪 Germany: Efficient, Predictable, Closed on Sundays

Germany operates on a different rhythm entirely. Shops close early — often by 8pm on weekdays, sometimes earlier. And on **Sundays, almost everything is closed**. Supermarkets, clothing stores, furniture shops — closed.

This is protected by law (*Ladenschlussgesetz*). It exists to protect workers' rest time, and most Germans support it. But for someone coming from Turkey, it takes serious adjustment.

What Germany does well: **price transparency and quality consistency**. Discounters like *Aldi* and *Lidl* offer genuinely good quality at low prices. *Rewe*, *Edeka*, and *Kaufland* cover the mid-range. There's no haggling, no surprises — the price on the shelf is the price you pay.

Weekly markets (*Wochenmarkt*) exist here too — and they're excellent. Fresh produce, local farmers, seasonal rhythms. In Munich especially, the *Viktualienmarkt* is a landmark worth visiting every week.

---

## ⚖️ The Real Comparison

| | Turkey | Germany |
|---|---|---|
| Opening hours | Long, flexible | Early close, Sunday shut |
| Sunday shopping | Fully open | Almost nothing open |
| Price negotiation | Common in markets | Not a thing |
| Price consistency | Unpredictable | Reliable |
| Fresh produce access | Excellent | Good (markets + shops) |
| Corner shop culture | Very strong | Limited |
| Online shopping | Good | Excellent (Amazon, Otto) |

---

## 💬 My Take

Sunday closures were genuinely hard at first. You learn to plan ahead — Friday shopping, stocked fridge, Sunday as an actual rest day. And after a while, you start to appreciate it. The city goes quiet. Families are out walking. There's a pace to it.

But I still miss the *bakkal*. That relationship-based, always-open corner shop culture is something Germany simply doesn't have — and probably never will.
