## Greece: Where History Meets Sunshine

Greece is both close and slightly challenging to reach for travelers — a Schengen visa is required. But once you get there, you understand why it's so popular. Sitting under the Acropolis in Athens, watching the sunset in Santorini or simply gazing at the blue of the Aegean is a completely different feeling.

## Athens: The Heart of History

### Acropolis and Parthenon
Coming to Greece without seeing the Acropolis is unthinkable. The 2,500-year-old Parthenon temple stands on a hill dominating the city. In the hours approaching sunset, the crowds thin and the light makes the view even more impressive.

**Entry fee:** 20 EUR (summer), 10 EUR (winter). Go early morning or late afternoon — midday is both hot and crowded.

### Acropolis Museum
Artifacts excavated from the Acropolis are displayed here. A world-class museum with modern design and a rich collection. Entry 10 EUR.

### Plaka Neighborhood
At the foot of the Acropolis, colorful buildings and narrow streets. Athens's most historic and tourist-friendly neighborhood. Ideal for shopping, food and coffee.

### Monastiraki Square and Market
An open-air market set up every Sunday morning. Antiques, books, souvenirs. Alongside it are the Monastiraki Mosque and structures from the Ottoman period.

### Syntagma Square
The parliament building and the famous Evzone guards. The changing of the guard ceremony that takes place on the hour is unmissable for photography enthusiasts.

## Islands: Which One to Visit?

| Island | Atmosphere | Crowds | Price |
|---|---|---|---|
| Santorini | Romantic, iconic | Very high | Expensive |
| Mykonos | Party, nightlife | Very high | Very expensive |
| Rhodes | History + sea | Medium | Medium |
| Crete | Large, has everything | Medium | Medium |
| Hydra | No vehicles, peaceful | Low | Medium |
| Paros | Sea, peaceful | Medium | Affordable |

**Recommendation:** Santorini and Mykonos look great in photos but are crowded and expensive in summer. Paros or Hydra offer a quieter and more authentic experience.

## Transportation

**Between islands:** Ferry is the most common option. Departures from Piraeus port. Flights to some islands are also available.

**Athens city transport:** Metro, bus and tram. With the Athens Card, all public transport is 9 EUR for 24 hours.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a visa required for Greece?**
Yes, a Schengen visa is required for Turkish citizens.

**How to get from Athens to Santorini?**
By plane 45 minutes (~50–100 EUR) or by ferry ~7 hours (fast ferry ~30 EUR).

**What is the best season for Greece?**
April–June and September–October. July–August is very hot and crowded.

**Is Athens dangerous?**
Generally safe. Be careful at Monastiraki market, pickpocketing incidents have been reported.
