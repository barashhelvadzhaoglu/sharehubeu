## Sarajevo: One of Europe's Most Moving Cities

When you arrive in Sarajevo, the first feeling is contradictory. On one side, the call to prayer and the Ottoman bazaar; on the other, church bells and bullet holes left from the war. Mosques, churches and synagogues stand side by side, just a few steps apart.

This city is both very familiar and unexpectedly deeply affecting for visitors.

## How to Get to Sarajevo?

**By plane:** Direct flights from Istanbul with THY, Pegasus and Anadolujet. Approximately 1.5 hours. Sarajevo International Airport is 6 km from the city.

**Airport to city:**
- Bus: Between 05:30–22:15, destination Baščaršija
- Taxi: Fixed fare, ~20 minutes

**City transport:** Tram, bus and trolleybus. Since Baščaršija is central, most places are within walking distance. You don't need a car to explore the city center — in fact, if you rent a car you might get stuck in narrow streets.

## Places to Visit

### 1. Baščaršija
The heart of Sarajevo. The bazaar dating from the Ottoman period, with its 15th–16th century texture still intact. Coffee shops, coppersmiths, souvenir shops in narrow cobblestone streets. Early morning or late afternoon — every hour has a different atmosphere.

**Sebilj Fountain:** In the middle of Baščaršija, the symbol of the city. An iconic photo spot with the surrounding pigeon flocks.

**Bosnian coffee:** Exactly the same as Turkish coffee but with a unique serving style. Small copper pot, served with Turkish delight and sugar.

### 2. Latin Bridge
Ordinary in appearance but historically incredible. It was right here at the head of this bridge in 1914 that Serbian nationalist Gavrilo Princip shot Archduke Franz Ferdinand, igniting the fuse of World War I. There is a small museum at the northern end of the bridge.

### 3. Gazi Husrev-beg Mosque
Right next to Baščaršija, built in 1531. Sarajevo's largest and most important Ottoman monument, built by Gazi Husrev-beg, a great-grandson of Fatih Sultan Mehmet.

### 4. Sarajevo Roses
You'll see flower-shaped marks painted in red on the streets. These are the traces left by bombs dropped during the war. The people of Sarajevo didn't want these marks erased — to not forget the war.

### 5. War Childhood Museum
A museum telling the story of the 1992–1995 Bosnian War through the eyes of children. One of Sarajevo's most moving museums. An exhibition that turns children's own stories into objects. You'll feel its effects long after visiting.

### 6. Sarajevo War Tunnel Museum
An 800-meter tunnel dug during the siege (1992–1995). This tunnel, which was the only access route to the besieged city, has now been turned into a museum. A bit outside the city but absolutely worth seeing.

### 7. Ferhadija Street and Surroundings
The pedestrian street. The area where Ottoman texture and Austro-Hungarian influence intertwine. At the end of the street is the Neo-Gothic Sarajevo Cathedral.

## Day Trip to Mostar

Going to Sarajevo without seeing Mostar is a big loss. About 2.5 hours by bus, 10–15 EUR per person.

**Must-sees in Mostar:**
- **Stari Most (Old Bridge):** UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by a student of the Ottoman architect Mimar Sinan, destroyed in the war, then rebuilt. Stands magnificently over the Neretva River.
- **Mostar Bazaar:** A smaller version of Sarajevo's Baščaršija
- **Kravica Waterfalls:** ~30 km from Mostar, a natural wonder

## Practical Information

**Money:** KM (Bosnian Mark). 1 EUR ≈ 1.95 KM. Souvenir shops generally cash only. Cards usually accepted at restaurants.

**Language:** Many shopkeepers in Baščaršija speak Turkish. Hearing Turkish conversations on the streets is not surprising.

**Safety:** Sarajevo is generally safe. Watch out for pickpockets around Baščaršija and on trams.

**Weather:** Very harsh winters. 30+ degrees in summer. Best period April–June and September–October.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need a visa for Bosnia Herzegovina?**
Turkish citizens can enter visa-free for up to 90 days with a burgundy or green passport.

**Is Turkish spoken in Sarajevo?**
Very common in the Baščaršija area. Among the older generation, there's Turkish knowledge left from the Ottoman period.

**How many days to see Sarajevo?**
2 days is sufficient for the city alone. 3–4 days is ideal with Mostar added.

**How to get to Mostar?**
Bus from Sarajevo ~2.5 hours, 10–15 EUR. Those going by car can also add Kravica Waterfalls to the route.

**Is Sarajevo expensive?**
Very affordable by European standards. A good meal 8–15 EUR, accommodation can be found between 30–80 EUR.
