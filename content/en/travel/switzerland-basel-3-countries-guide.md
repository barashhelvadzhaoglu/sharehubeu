## Basel: One City, Three Countries

Basel's most interesting feature is its geographical location. It stands at the exact intersection of Switzerland, France and Germany. From the city, it's 45 minutes to the French city of Colmar and 30 minutes to the German city of Freiburg. There's a single airport used by all three countries: EuroAirport.

This means: someone flying to Basel can see Switzerland, France and Germany in the same trip.

## Airport: Three Separate Exits to Three Countries

At EuroAirport Basel-Mulhouse-Freiburg, pay attention when exiting the gate: There are three different exits — Switzerland, France (Mulhouse) and Germany (Freiburg). Whichever exit you pass through, you're considered to have entered that country. Most car rental offices are on the Swiss side.

## Places to Visit in Basel

### 1. Marktplatz (Market Square)
The historic heart of the city. In the middle stands the Town Hall (Rathaus), built of red sandstone with a history of over 500 years. A market is set up in the morning — seasonal fruits, cheeses and local products. A Christmas market is set up here in November–December, one of Europe's most beautiful.

### 2. Basel Minster (Münster)
A Protestant cathedral dating from the 9th century. It defines the city's silhouette with its red-tiled roof and twin towers. On the Swiss National Heritage list. Even walking around it is enjoyable thanks to its commanding position overlooking the river.

### 3. Rhine Riverside
The Rhine River (Rhein) divides the city into two: historic Greater Basel and modern Lesser Basel. Five bridges connect the two banks. In summer, locals swim in the river — they're carried from one bank to the other thanks to the strong current. Even watching is entertaining.

**Tip:** Drinking water flows from the fountains along the river. Don't pay, fill up your thermos.

### 4. Kunstmuseum Basel (Art Museum)
One of Switzerland's most important and one of the world's select art museums. Comprehensive collection from Renaissance to modern art. It's said that Basel earns its nickname of "Switzerland's cultural capital" thanks to this museum. Entry is paid, free with the Swiss Travel Pass.

### 5. Three Countries Corner (Dreiländereck)
A bit outside the city but worth seeing. A bridge and monument where France, Germany and Switzerland can be seen simultaneously. The "Triangle of Borders" monument, built from an architectural competition held in 1990, stands here. Perfect for photos — three country flags side by side.

### 6. Art Basel Period (June)
Every year in June, the world's most prestigious contemporary art fair takes place in Basel. During this period, the city takes on a completely different energy. Tickets to the fair are expensive, but outdoor artworks around the city can be seen for free.

## Day Trip 3-Country Route from Basel

It's possible to see three countries on the same day using Basel as a base.

### France Side: Alsace Route

| Place | Distance | Transport |
|---|---|---|
| Colmar | ~45 min | Train |
| Strasbourg | ~1 hour | Train |
| Alsace villages (Riquewihr, Eguisheim) | ~1 hour | Car |

**Colmar:** Colorful half-timbered houses, canals, medieval texture. A magical atmosphere during Christmas. It's said to have inspired the village in Disney's Beauty and the Beast.

**Strasbourg:** The headquarters of the European Parliament and Council of Europe. Referred to as "the capital of Europe." Notre-Dame Cathedral and the Petite France neighborhood are must-sees.

### Germany Side

| Place | Distance | Transport |
|---|---|---|
| Freiburg | ~30 min | Train |
| Black Forest (Schwarzwald) | ~1 hour | Car |
| Europa Park | ~45 min | Car |

**Freiburg:** University city, young and lively atmosphere. Small canals (Bächle) running through the city center offer an interesting sight.

**Black Forest (Schwarzwald):** The legendary forested mountain region in southwestern Germany. Hiking routes, villages, and Germany's best Black Forest cake come from here.

## Food and Drink

Basel's cuisine is a combination of Swiss, French and German.

| Food | Description | Note |
|---|---|---|
| Basler Läckerli | Spiced cookie, the city's iconic sweet | Can also be bought as a souvenir |
| Zwiebelwähe | Onion tart | Typical Swiss taste |
| Fondue | Swiss classic | Ideal for dinner |
| Flammkuchen | Thin-crust tart, Alsace influence | A taste coming from the French border |

**Important note:** Basel prices are as high as Zurich. Getting lunch from Coop or Migros provides great savings. A simple meal at a restaurant is 20–35 CHF.

**Money tip:** Finding a currency exchange bureau is difficult. Paying by card is much more practical. If you pay with Euros, the change will come in CHF and the exchange rate will work against you.

## Accommodation

Accommodation in Basel is expensive by Swiss standards. But some hotels give a **Basel Card** — with this card, city public transport is free.

Alternative: Staying in Freiburg or Colmar and coming to Basel for a day trip. Accommodation in Germany and France is much more affordable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can I cross to France and Germany with a single visa from Basel?**
Yes. The Schengen visa is valid in all Schengen countries. France and Germany are also in the Schengen area.

**How to get from the airport to the city?**
Exit from the Swiss side and get on bus number 50. You'll reach the city center in ~30 minutes.

**Are there Turkish speakers in Basel?**
Among the most numerous foreign residents in Basel are Turks. But general communication is in English.

**When is Art Basel?**
Generally in mid-June, lasting 1 week.

**When is the Basel Christmas market?**
Late November – mid-December. The markets at Barfüsserplatz and Münsterplatz are especially famous.
